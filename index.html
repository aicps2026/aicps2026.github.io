    Pr. José Maria Cecilia

    Technical University of Valencia, Spain

    Abstract

    Environmental Digital Twins and Edge AI for the Intelligent Management of Vulnerable Ecosystems

    The increasing environmental and climatic pressures on fragile ecosystems—such as coastal lagoons, wetlands, and protected areas—demand innovative strategies for monitoring, analysis, and decision-making. This keynote will present insights and results from two research projects—SMARTLAGOON and CHAN-TWIN—that have developed pioneering solutions based on Environmental Digital Twins, Edge Artificial Intelligence (Edge AI), and Participatory Data Science to support the sustainable management of the Mar Menor lagoon and the Torrevieja and La Mata lagoons (Alicante, Spain). The talk will demonstrate how to integrate physical data (GNSS-IR sensors, intelligent cameras, meteorological stations), social data (social sensing from online platforms and citizen participation), and predictive models (AI, time-series forecasting, environmental simulation) into a unified digital twin that empowers both citizens and public authorities to make informed and adaptive decisions. Finally, the keynote will reflect on the current challenges in building sustainable, interoperable, and scalable digital infrastructures for environmental management, and discuss how these methodologies can be transferred and replicated in other ecologically and socially valuable contexts.

    Pr. Abdeldjalil Ouahabi

    TOURS University

    Abstract

    Exploring the Frontiers of Artificial Intelligence: Innovations, Applications, and Ethical Considerations

    In recent years, AI advances have accelerated dramatically, thanks to progress in deep learning, generative models, and the availability of vast amounts of data. These systems can now generate text, create realistic images, compose music, contribute to medical diagnosis, and even aid in scientific research. However, this rapid evolution raises crucial ethical and regulatory questions. How can we ensure that AI remains safe, transparent, and respectful of human values? Policymakers worldwide are still striving to find the right balance: insufficient regulation could lead to abuses, while excessive control could stifle innovation and limit the benefits AI can bring to society, such as improving healthcare or addressing global challenges. This presentation reviews the fundamentals of AI and deep learning, explores practical applications such as facial recognition and medical imaging, and addresses the ethical considerations that must guide the responsible development and deployment of intelligent systems.

    Pr. Amit Kumar Singh

    University of Essex, UK

    Abstract

    Secure, Sustainable and Efficient: Designing the Digital Infrastructure of Tomorrow

    As digital systems become the backbone of global innovation, the challenge before us is clear: how do we design infrastructure that is not only technologically advanced, but also secure, sustainable, and efficient—across borders, sectors, and scales. This keynote addresses the urgent need for a new paradigm in digital infrastructure design. We begin with security, exploring global strategies for resilience against cyber threats and the future of trust in a hyperconnected world. We then turn to sustainability, examining how data centers, cloud platforms, and hardware ecosystems can align with climate goals through energy optimization, carbon-aware computing, and responsible sourcing. Finally, we explore efficiency, highlighting innovations in AI-driven automation, edge computing, and intelligent orchestration that enable scalable, low-latency systems. Through real-world examples and forward-looking strategies, this talk challenges technologists, policymakers, and industry leaders to rethink infrastructure as a dynamic ecosystem—one that must be secure by design, sustainable by necessity/commitment, and efficient by innovation.

    Mr. Toufik DJERADI

    Sensia an SLB & Rockwell Automation JV, USA

    Abstract

    Smart Oilfields: AI-Driven Edge Computing and Response Prioritization for Sustainable Production

    In the evolving landscape of digital oilfield operations, Sensia introduces a transformative approach to artificial lift optimization through its Hyperconverged Edge Controller (HCC2) and AI Response Prioritization (AiRP) system. The HCC2 serves as a robust, scalable edge computing platform designed to unify sensing, intelligence, and control at the wellsite, enabling real-time data processing and decision-making closer to the source. Complementing this, AiRP leverages advanced artificial intelligence and machine learning algorithms to automate incident detection and prioritization for ESPs. By distinguishing critical events from routine alerts, AiRP significantly reduces response times—from hours to minutes—enhancing operational efficiency, minimizing downtime, and improving asset performance. Together, these technologies exemplify Sensia’s commitment to digitalization, offering oil and gas operators a pathway to smarter, safer, and more sustainable production.

    Pr. Houbing Herbert Song

    University of Maryland, USA

    Abstract

    Neuro-symbolic AI: The Third Wave of AI

    There are three waves of Artificial Intelligence. The first Wave of AI is Crafted Knowledge, which includes rule-based AI systems. The second wave of AI is Statistical Learning, which includes machine becoming intelligent by using statistical methods. The third wave of AI is contextual adaptation. In the third wave, instead of learning from data, intelligent machines will understand and perceive the world on its own, and learn by understanding the world and reason with it. Neuro-symbolic AI, which combines neural networks with symbolic representations, has emerged as a promising solution of the third wave of AI. In this talk, first I will share my journey from counter-unmanned aircraft systems to explainable AI to neuro-symbolic AI. Then I will present the past, present, and future of neuro-symbolic AI. I will also share my perspective on the emerging area of neuro-symbolic AI.

    CFP

    Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS) are at the forefront of technological innovation, transforming industries and revolutionizing critical sectors. AI, with its ability to process vast amounts of data and learn from patterns, is enhancing decision-making, automating complex tasks, and driving efficiency. CPS, which integrates physical systems with digital computing, enables real-time monitoring, control, and optimization of processes in diverse applications. Together, AI and CPS are reshaping the way industries operate, enhancing safety, efficiency, and resilience in our interconnected world.

    AICPS'2025 aims to bring together researchers, academics, engineers, and industry professionals in the fields of AI and CPS to share groundbreaking work and explore the latest advancements. AICPS'2025 invites participants to submit original, unpublished papers on topics related to the conference, including but not limited to:

    • IoT Ecosystem: From Devices to Edge and Cloud Integration

      Explores the interconnectedness of IoT devices, edge computing, and cloud platforms, focusing on data flow, security, and scalability.

    • Advanced Sensors and Sensing Technologies

      Covers cutting-edge sensor technologies, including their design, fabrication, and applications in various fields, such as healthcare, environment, and industry.

    • Innovative Circuits and Systems for AI and IoT Applications

      Discusses the development of efficient and low-power circuits and systems to support AI and IoT applications, including hardware accelerators and embedded systems.

    • Next-Generation Communications, Connectivity, and Networking

      Explores emerging technologies like 5G, 6G, and beyond, focusing on high-speed data transmission, low-latency communication, and network security.

    • Big Data, Data Engineering, and Advanced Analytics

      Covers techniques for processing, analyzing, and extracting insights from large and complex datasets, including data mining, machine learning, and data visualization.

    • Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Cognitive Computing

      Explores the development and application of AI techniques, such as deep learning, natural language processing, and computer vision, to solve complex problems.

    • Blockchain and Distributed Ledger Technologies

      Discusses the principles, applications, and challenges of blockchain technology, including its use in finance, supply chain management, and healthcare.

    • Design Automation in the Era of AI & IoT: From Silicon to Systems

      Explores the use of AI and automation tools to design complex integrated circuits and systems, accelerating the development of innovative products.

    • Cybersecurity and Privacy Solutions

      Covers the latest techniques and strategies for protecting digital systems and data from cyber threats, including encryption, intrusion detection, and privacy-preserving technologies.

    • Intelligent Robotics and Autonomous Systems

      Explores the development of intelligent robots and autonomous systems, including their applications in manufacturing, healthcare, and transportation.

    • Emerging Applications, Processes, and Digital Services

      Discusses innovative applications and services enabled by emerging technologies, such as the Internet of Things, artificial intelligence, and blockchain.

    • Advanced Computer Architectures and Embedded Systems

      Explores the design and optimization of computer architectures and embedded systems for high-performance and energy-efficient computing.

    • Bio-Informatics

      Covers the application of computational techniques to analyze and interpret biological data, including genomics, proteomics, and bioinformatics.
